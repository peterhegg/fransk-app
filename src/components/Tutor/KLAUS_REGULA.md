# Präzise Wärme — Klaus & Regula

*A character-design philosophy for the Swiss-German tutor personas, expressed in the same engraved-line language as Le Tuteur (Henri/Simone).*

## The movement

**Präzise Wärme** — "precise warmth." Where the French tutors carry the smoke and melancholy of the Left Bank, the Swiss tutors carry the quiet exactness of a Zürich lecture hall: a watchmaker's restraint warmed by alpine light. Nothing decorative survives that isn't load-bearing. Every stroke is the product of measured intent, drawn as though by an engraver who has spent decades removing lines until only the essential ones remain. The figure should read as meticulously calibrated — clarity mistaken for simplicity only by those who haven't tried to draw it.

## Form and line

Same 1.6 px engraving stroke, same elongated head, same floating figure — the two language families must feel like plates from one book. The Swiss identity lives in three deliberate substitutions, each placed with painstaking care so the silhouette differs at thumbnail scale, not just on inspection. The half-moon reader gives way to the **full round modernist frame** — Bauhaus-by-way-of-Le-Corbusier, the eyes sitting *within* the lens rather than peering over it: engaged, present, exact. Hair abandons the romantic sweep for the **clean side-part** (Klaus) and the **architectural bob** (Regula) — combed, intentional, never tousled.

## The faces

**Klaus** wears a **short, trimmed beard** that hugs the jaw — neat as a hedge in a Swiss courtyard, the opposite of bohemian stubble. It frames a dignified, faintly amused mouth. He is the professor who insists, gently, that German is *logical*, and means it. **Regula** — named for Zürich's patron saint, a reference only locals will catch — keeps the chin-length bob, a turtleneck under the same tweed, an economy of line that reads as composure rather than coldness.

## Color and warmth

The palette is the app's antrasitt-and-amber: a near-black ink (`#1c1f26`) carrying the strokes, an amber (`#d4862a`) reserved for the rare accent — a glint of warmth in an otherwise tempered field, like late sun on grey stone. Warmth is *earned*, never spilled. The line stays monochrome currentColor so the figure inherits whichever ink the theme provides.

## Craft

This must look labored over — the work of someone at the top of the field who redrew the bridge of the glasses forty times to get the arch to clear the nose without touching it. The reward is sustained: the casual viewer sees a calm Swiss tutor; the careful one sees that not a single curve is accidental. Master-level execution, hidden inside apparent ease.
